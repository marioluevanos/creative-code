What makes a "hero" a "hero?"

The hero section aims to make a solid first impression that conveys the site's or page content's essence, engages users, and encourages them to explore further.
A hero should be functionally robust, fast-loading, and accessible across all devices.

From a design perspective:

What makes a "hero" a "hero?"

  • Prominent Placement
  • Visual Focus
  • Minimal Content
  • Size and Scale
  • Prominent Placement: Designed to be one of the first things users see.
  • Visual Focus: This section usually includes large, high-quality images or videos that are visually striking and intended to capture the user's attention immediately.
  • Minimal Content: The hero area often contains a minimal amount of text, such as a headline or a brief description, which communicates the site's core message or value proposition. Call to Action: It frequently includes a call-to-action (CTA) button or link that guides users toward taking a desired action, such as signing up, learning more, or viewing a product.
  • Size and Scale: The hero section can cover the entire screen (full-screen hero) or a large part of the viewport, making it a dominant element on the page.
